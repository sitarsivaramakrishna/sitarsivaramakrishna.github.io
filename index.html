About the Artist

✽ ✽ ✽

Basavaraj Sivaramakrishna Rao, fondly called Sitar Siva by friends, is a distinguished sitarist and composer who hails from a family of musicians. Initiated into the niceties of music at the tender age of seven by his father B. Sudarsana Rao, he went on to become a disciple of the illustrious and veteran sitarist, late Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan of the Mian Achpal and Senia Gharana.

The melodious and mellifluous music that he brings out through the Sitar illustrates his strong creative imagination that scales great heights. Uniquely versatile, he is equally at home with the Hindustani classical idiom of alaap, jod, jhala and gath as with the Carnatic alapana, kriti and kalpanaswaras — a rare mastery among sitarists.

He has performed extensively across the globe — in the USA, Canada, France, Germany, Switzerland, Australia, China, Sri Lanka, South Africa, Mauritius, and the Middle East — gracing prestigious stages from the Melbourne Arts Festival and Drive East Festival in New York to the German Embassy in Berlin and Stanford University.

As the founder of Aruna Music Entertainment, he produces and composes music for artists across genres, and continues to teach the next generation through his SRK Academy of Music.

Guru & Lineage

✽ ✽ ✽

The sacred tradition of Guru-Shishya Parampara

Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan — Sitar

Sitar Nawaz Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan hails from the ancient and famous Mian Achpal and Senia Gharana, which has made great contributions to the preservation, promotion, and development of the high traditions of Hindustani Classical Music.

He achieved such high perfection and unsurpassed mastery on the Sitar that he could make it sing, cry, wail, and even talk — "The blood of music in his veins."

Ustad ji had his initial training from his father Ustad Bundu Khan Saheb and later studied under Ustad Rahimat Khan Saheb and Ustad Habib Khan Sahib. He became an exponent of the special techniques of Raza Khani, Maseet Khani, and Seni Bhaj. Originally from Pune, he moved to Madras and served as a teaching faculty at the Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan, Madras, training many aspirants.

Basavaraj Brothers

✽ ✽ ✽

Where Hindustani meets Carnatic — Sitar & Violin in Jugalbandi

A Rare Jugalbandi

The Basavaraj Brothers — B. Sivaramakrishna Rao on Sitar and B.V. Raghavendra Rao on Violin — present a unique confluence of North Indian Hindustani and South Indian Carnatic classical music traditions.

Their Jugalbandi (duet) performances seamlessly weave together the Sitar and Violin, supported by both Tabla and Mridangam, creating a musical experience where two great traditions meet, converse, and celebrate their shared heritage.

They have performed globally and collaborated with artists like guitarist Nadaka, releasing albums such as Living Colours and Live in Paris.

Ateetam

✽ ✽ ✽

"The Beyond" — a journey into the inner worlds of beauty and calm

Song of the Soul

Ateetam — from the Sanskrit word meaning "The Beyond" — is a musical project that draws deep from the wells of Indian classical tradition to create meditative, spiritual soundscapes.

Deep calls from the bamboo flute and enticing vibrations from the sitar draw you into the inner worlds of beauty and calm, supported by the rhythmic pulse of tabla and the ambient textures of keyboard.

Albums include Spirit of India, Song of the Soul, Tracing Beyond, and Incredible India.

SRK Academy of Music

✽ ✽ ✽

Preserving and passing on the tradition of the Sitar

With decades of performance and teaching experience, B. Sivaramakrishna Rao continues the sacred Guru-Shishya Parampara through his SRK Academy of Music, nurturing students in India and across the world.

The academy offers training in both Hindustani Classical and Carnatic Classical music on the Sitar — a rare offering that reflects the artist's own unique mastery of both traditions.

  • One-on-one personalized sitar instruction
  • Both Hindustani & Carnatic classical traditions
  • Online classes for students worldwide
  • In-person classes in Bangalore, India
  • Suitable for beginners to advanced students
  • Guidance on sitar selection, tuning & maintenance

Learn To Play Sitar — Vol. 1

Instructional DVD covering the history of the sitar, types of sitar, handling, tuning techniques, basic lessons, fingering techniques, and concert patterns. A comprehensive guide for beginners.

Learn To Play Sitar — Vol. 2
