# Website Summary — sitarsiva.in

## Overview

**sitarsiva.in** is the official website of **B. Sivaramakrishna Rao**, a distinguished Indian classical sitarist, composer, and music educator based in Bangalore, India. Fondly called "Sitar Siva" by friends, he is a disciple of the late Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan of the Mian Achpal and Senia Gharana. The website serves as a comprehensive digital presence showcasing his career as a performer, composer, and guru.

## Site Structure

The website is organized into five pages:

### Homepage (sitarsiva.in)
The landing page opens with a split-layout hero featuring a solo portrait photo on the left and the Sanskrit invocation *"sarasvatI namastubhyam"* on the right, introducing the artist as "Sitarist, Composer, Guru." An **Explore** section presents three pathways into the site — The Sitarist, Aruna Music Entertainment (his music label), and SRK Academy of Music (his teaching academy). The page also features a detailed **biography**, **Press & Reviews** with coverage from The Hindu, Deccan Herald, Times of India, and international publications, and a **Contact** form for bookings, collaborations, and lesson inquiries.

### The Sitarist (sitarsiva.in/sitarist.html)
This page showcases the artist's performing career across multiple dimensions:

- **Streaming Links** — Prominently displayed in the hero content area with direct links to 20+ albums on Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, JioSaavn, and YouTube.
- **Albums** — Featured album videos: *Carnatic on Sitar* and *Healing Ragas*, displayed side by side.
- **Raga Dictionary** — A curated collection of ragas with recordings, each linking to YouTube performances. Ragas include Bageshri, Bhairav, Darbari Kanada, Malkauns, Yaman, and many more.
- **Solo Sitar — Classical** — Video performances organized into Hindustani Classical (3 videos), Carnatic on Sitar, Fusion & Covers, and Devotional & Compositions.
- **Basavaraj Brothers** — The celebrated duo of B. Sivaramakrishna Rao (Sitar) and B.V. Raghavendra Rao (Violin), performing Jugalbandi concerts that bridge Hindustani and Carnatic traditions. Six concert videos are featured.
- **Ateetam** — A meditative music project meaning "The Beyond" in Sanskrit. Albums include *Spirit of India*, *Song of the Soul*, and *Tracing Beyond*.

### Composer (sitarsiva.in/composer.html)
Showcases the artist's work as founder of **Aruna Music Entertainment (AME)**, a distinguished South Indian production house founded by Aruna Sivaramakrishna Rao:

- **About** — Introduction to AME with its vision ("tradition and innovation converge") and practice areas.
- **Projects** — Five productions ordered newest-first:
  - *Jai Jai Dattatreya* — Grand music and dance production in collaboration with IOAA (Austin, Texas).
  - *Geeth Govind* (2020–2023) — Landmark collaboration with Invis Multimedia presenting all 24 Ashtapadis of Jayadeva.
  - *Heroes' Might — Veershakti* (September 2021) — Musical play from Une Production Florent Paris-Berlin.
  - *Jay Rang Rang — Kashi La Lumière* (March 2021) — Spiritual composition, Une Production Florent Paris-Berlin.
  - *Essence of Life & The Art of Meditation* (2011) — Conceptual production inspired by J. Krishnamurti philosophy. Produced by Dega Arts.
- **Gallery** — 40+ photographs from concerts, collaborations, and international tours, filterable by category.

### Guru (sitarsiva.in/guru.html)
Dedicated to the sacred Guru-Shishya Parampara (teacher-student tradition):

- **Guru & Lineage** — Opens with *"Sri Gurubhyo Namaha"*. A tribute to Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan and the Mian Achpal and Senia Gharana. The lineage tree traces from the legendary Beenkar Bande Ali Khan, through Ustad Rahimat Khan (1863-1954), to Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan, and to B. Sivaramakrishna Rao as the present torchbearer.
- **The Academy** — Personalized one-on-one sitar instruction (online and in-person in Bangalore/Chennai). Course options for Beginner (12 classes/quarter) and Advanced/Professional (13/11/9 classes/quarter). Full Terms & Conditions listed.
- **Student Performances** — 2 student performance videos displayed horizontally.
- **Learn to Play Sitar** — 2 instructional DVD videos (*Learn To Play Sitar* Vol. 1 & 2) displayed horizontally.
- **Testimonials** — 4 student review images.

### Moments (sitarsiva.in/moments.html)
Photo gallery organized by category with lightbox viewer:

- **With Artists** — Photos with celebrated musicians (Hariharan, Ustad Shahid Parvez, Ustad Rashid Khan, Bombay Jayashree, Dr. Balamuralikrishna, Devi Sri Prasad, and others).
- **With Students** — 4 photos with students (centered layout).
- **Concert Pictures** — Concert photos including 7 former hero background images.
- **International** — Photos from international performances and tours.
- **Awards** — 2 award photos (Madhura Murali Puraskar, Nada Chintamani Award), centered.

## Key Highlights

- **Awards**: "Child Prodigy of the Year" (1990, Governor of Tamil Nadu); Madhura Murali Puraskar (from Dr. M. Balamuralikrishna)
- **Discography**: 20+ albums spanning classical, devotional, fusion, meditation, and healing music
- **International Reach**: Performed in 15+ countries including USA, Canada, France, Germany, Switzerland, Australia, China, Sri Lanka, South Africa, and Mauritius
- **Unique Mastery**: Equally proficient in both Hindustani (alaap, jod, jhala, gath) and Carnatic (alapana, kriti, kalpanaswaras) idioms on the sitar — a rare distinction among sitarists
- **Labels**: Super Audio, RPG Saregama, Rain Tree Records, Geethanjali Records, Moserbaer, Dega Arts

## Technical Details

The website is a static site built with plain HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript — no frameworks or build tools. It is hosted on GitHub Pages with a custom domain (sitarsiva.in). The design uses a warm Indian classical aesthetic with a maroon, gold, and cream color palette, Playfair Display and Lora typography, Sanskrit ornamental elements, and fade-in scroll animations. All images are optimized in WebP format with lazy loading. The site is fully responsive across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. Heroes use a split-layout design with solo portrait photos on the left and text content on the right, with 8 photos rotating randomly on each page load.
