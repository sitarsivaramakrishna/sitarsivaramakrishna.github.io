# Website Summary — sitarsiva.in

## Overview

**sitarsiva.in** is the official website of **B. Sivaramakrishna Rao**, a distinguished Indian classical sitarist, composer, and music educator based in Bangalore, India. Fondly called "Sitar Siva" by friends, he is a disciple of the late Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan of the Mian Achpal and Senia Gharana. The website serves as a comprehensive digital presence showcasing his career as a performer, composer, and guru.

## Site Structure

The website is organized into four pages:

### Homepage (sitarsiva.in)
The landing page opens with a full-screen hero featuring the Sanskrit invocation *"sarasvatI namastubhyam"* and introduces the artist as "Sitarist, Composer, Guru." An **Explore** section presents three pathways into the site — The Sitarist, Aruna Music Entertainment (his music label), and SRK Academy of Music (his teaching academy). The page also features an **Upcoming Concerts** section, a detailed **biography**, **Press & Reviews** with coverage from The Hindu, Deccan Herald, Times of India, and international publications, and a **Contact** form for bookings, collaborations, and lesson inquiries.

### The Sitarist (sitarsiva.in/sitarist.html)
This page showcases the artist's performing career across multiple dimensions:

- **Raga Dictionary** — A curated collection of 29 ragas with 60+ recordings, each linking to YouTube performances. Ragas include Bageshri, Bhairav, Darbari Kanada, Malkauns, Yaman, and many more.
- **Solo Sitar — Classical** — Video performances organized into Hindustani Classical, Carnatic on Sitar, Fusion & Covers, and Devotional & Compositions. Notable performances include Raag Desh, Healing Ragas, A.R. Rahman Medley on Sitar, and collaborations with artists like K.S. Chitra and Lydian Nadhaswaram.
- **Basavaraj Brothers** — The celebrated duo of B. Sivaramakrishna Rao (Sitar) and B.V. Raghavendra Rao (Violin), performing Jugalbandi concerts that bridge Hindustani and Carnatic traditions. Six concert videos are featured, including performances in Paris, New York, and Hyderabad.
- **Ateetam** — A meditative music project meaning "The Beyond" in Sanskrit, featuring Sitar, Flute/Bansuri (Shakthidhar), Tabla (Venky D.C.), and Keyboard (Varun Pradeep). Albums include *Spirit of India*, *Song of the Soul*, and *Tracing Beyond*.
- **Streaming Links** — Direct links to 20+ albums on Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, JioSaavn, and YouTube.

### Composer (sitarsiva.in/composer.html)
Showcases the artist's work as founder of **Aruna Music Entertainment (AME)**, a distinguished South Indian production house founded by Aruna Sivaramakrishna Rao:

- **About** — Introduction to AME with its vision ("tradition and innovation converge") and practice areas: artistic production, digital publishing, music development, international collaborations, and live concert experiences.
- **Projects** — Five productions ordered newest-first:
  - *Jai Jai Dattatreya* — Grand music and dance production in collaboration with IOAA (Austin, Texas), filmed in Pushkar, Rajasthan with 60+ dancers, exploring a soul's journey in search of a Guru.
  - *Geeth Govind* (2020–2023) — Landmark collaboration with Invis Multimedia presenting all 24 Ashtapadis of Jayadeva in Hindustani-contemporary idiom, featuring Kathak by Dr. Guru Pali Chandra.
  - *Heroes' Might — Veershakti* (September 2021) — Evocative musical play from Une Production Florent Paris-Berlin, exploring the duality of motherhood through dialogues between a mother and son.
  - *Jay Rang Rang — Kashi La Lumière* (March 2021) — Spiritual composition born during meditation on the sacred evening of Devashayani Ekadashi (2017) in Berlin, a collaboration with Une Production Florent Paris-Berlin.
  - *Essence of Life & The Art of Meditation* (2011) — Conceptual production inspired by J. Krishnamurti philosophy, integrating Bharatanatyam, Kuchipudi, Mohiniyattam, Odissi, and Kathak, with vocals by Dr. M. Balamuralikrishna, Hariharan, and Sanjay Abhyankar. Produced by Dega Arts.
- **Gallery** — 40+ photographs from concerts, collaborations, and international tours, filterable by category (Concerts, With Artists, International, Awards). Photos document performances at the German Embassy in Berlin, Stanford University, Google San Francisco, Melbourne Arts Festival, Drive East Festival New York, and many more venues. Notable collaborations captured include those with Padmashree Hariharan, Ustad Shahid Parvez, Ustad Rashid Khan, Bombay Jayashree, Sivamani, Dr. M. Balamuralikrishna, and Music Director Devi Sri Prasad.

### Guru (sitarsiva.in/guru.html)
Dedicated to the sacred Guru-Shishya Parampara (teacher-student tradition):

- **Guru & Lineage** — A tribute to Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan and the Mian Achpal and Senia Gharana. The lineage tree traces from the legendary Beenkar Bande Ali Khan, through Ustad Rahimat Khan (1863-1954, credited with adding the kharaj-shadaj string to the sitar), to Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan, and to B. Sivaramakrishna Rao as the present torchbearer.
- **The Academy** — Information about the SRK Academy of Music, offering one-on-one sitar instruction in both Hindustani and Carnatic classical traditions, available online worldwide and in-person in Bangalore. Features two instructional DVDs (*Learn To Play Sitar* Vol. 1 & 2) and student performance videos.
- **Testimonials** — Coming soon.

## Key Highlights

- **Awards**: "Child Prodigy of the Year" (1990, Governor of Tamil Nadu); Madhura Murali Puraskar (from Dr. M. Balamuralikrishna)
- **Discography**: 20+ albums spanning classical, devotional, fusion, meditation, and healing music
- **International Reach**: Performed in 15+ countries including USA, Canada, France, Germany, Switzerland, Australia, China, Sri Lanka, South Africa, and Mauritius
- **Unique Mastery**: Equally proficient in both Hindustani (alaap, jod, jhala, gath) and Carnatic (alapana, kriti, kalpanaswaras) idioms on the sitar — a rare distinction among sitarists
- **Labels**: Super Audio, RPG Saregama, Rain Tree Records, Geethanjali Records, Moserbaer, Dega Arts

## Technical Details

The website is a static site built with plain HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript — no frameworks or build tools. It is hosted on GitHub Pages with a custom domain (sitarsiva.in). The design uses a warm Indian classical aesthetic with a maroon, gold, and cream color palette, Playfair Display and Lora typography, Sanskrit ornamental elements, and fade-in scroll animations. All images are optimized in WebP format with lazy loading. The site is fully responsive across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
