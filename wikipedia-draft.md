**Basavaraj Sivaramakrishna Rao** (also known as **Sitar Siva**) is an Indian [[sitar]]ist, composer, and music educator based in [[Bangalore]], India. He is a disciple of [[Ustad]] Ahmed Hussain Khan of the Mian Achpal and [[Senia gharana]].<ref name="hindu2005">{{cite news |title=Chennai Music Season Review |newspaper=[[The Hindu]] |date=December 2005 |location=Chennai}}</ref> He is known for his rare proficiency in performing both [[Hindustani classical music|Hindustani]] and [[Carnatic music|Carnatic]] classical music on the sitar, and is the founder of Aruna Music Entertainment and the SRK Academy of Music.

== Early life and training ==

Sivaramakrishna Rao hails from a family of musicians. He was initiated into music at the age of seven by his father, B. Sudarsana Rao.[citation needed] He subsequently became a disciple of the veteran sitarist Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan of the Mian Achpal and [[Senia gharana]], an ancient lineage that has made significant contributions to the preservation and development of Hindustani classical music.[citation needed]

In 1990, he was awarded the title "Child Prodigy of the Year" by the [[Governor of Tamil Nadu]] at the Madras Telugu Academy Ugadi Puraskar ceremony.[citation needed]

== Gharana lineage ==

Sivaramakrishna Rao belongs to the Mian Achpal and Senia Gharana. His musical lineage traces through:

* '''[[Bande Ali Khan]]''' — legendary [[veena|beenkar]]
* '''Ustad Rahimat Khan''' (1863–1954) — a contemporary of [[Vishnu Narayan Bhatkhande|Bhatkande]] and [[Vishnu Digambar Paluskar|Paluskar]], who founded eight music schools and is credited with adding the ''kharaj-shadaj'' string to the sitar, establishing the ''kharaj-pancham''-string setup widely used today[citation needed]
* '''Ustad Ahmed Hussain Khan''' — trained by his father Ustad Bundu Khan Saheb, and later by Ustad Rahimat Khan Saheb and Ustad Habib Khan Sahib; became an exponent of ''Raza Khani'', ''Maseet Khani'', and ''Seni Bhaj'' techniques; served as teaching faculty at [[Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan]], Madras[citation needed]
* '''B. Sivaramakrishna Rao''' — present torchbearer of the gharana

== Musical career ==

=== Solo career ===

Sivaramakrishna Rao is noted for his versatility in performing both Hindustani and Carnatic classical music on the sitar. A review in ''[[The Hindu]]'' during the Chennai Music Season (December 2005) observed that "Sivaramakrishna was as dexterous with ''alapana'', ''kriti'', and ''kalpanaswaras'' as he was with ''alaap'', ''jod'', ''jhala'' and ''gath''... the stage sounded perfectly orchestrated."<ref name="hindu2005"/>

He has released over 20 albums across genres including classical, devotional, fusion, meditation, and healing music. Notable albums include ''Carnatic In Sitar'' ([[Saregama|RPG Saregama]], 2007), the ''Healing Ragas'' series, ''Spiritual Music of India'', and ''Music from Sacred Temples'', released through labels including Super Audio, RPG Saregama, Moserbaer, Geethanjali Records, and his own Aruna Music Entertainment.[citation needed]

=== Basavaraj Brothers ===

Together with his brother B.V. Raghavendra Rao ([[violin]]ist), Sivaramakrishna Rao performs as the '''Basavaraj Brothers'''. The duo presents [[jugalbandi]] concerts that combine Hindustani and Carnatic classical traditions, featuring sitar and violin supported by both [[tabla]] and [[mridangam]].[citation needed]

The Basavaraj Brothers have collaborated with French guitarist [[Nadaka (musician)|Nadaka]], releasing the albums ''Living Colours'' and ''Live in Paris'' on Rain Tree Records.[citation needed] They have performed internationally, including concerts in Paris, New York, Basel (Switzerland), and a 2024 European tour under the patronage of the Embassy of India in France.[citation needed]

=== Ateetam ===

Sivaramakrishna Rao is a founding member of '''Ateetam''' (from the [[Sanskrit]] word meaning "The Beyond"), a meditative and spiritual music project. The ensemble includes Shakthidhar (flute/[[bansuri]]), Venkatesh D.C. (tabla/percussion), and Varun Pradeep (keyboard). Albums include ''Spirit of India'' (2015), ''Song of the Soul'' (2016), and ''Tracing Beyond'' (2017). The ''Ateetam'' album was released by [[M. Balamuralikrishna|Dr. M. Balamuralikrishna]].[citation needed]

=== Aruna Music Entertainment ===

Sivaramakrishna Rao founded '''Aruna Music Entertainment''', a music production label based in Bangalore. The label produces and releases devotional, classical, fusion, and meditation music. Notable productions include ''Jay Rang Rang — Kashi La Lumiere'' (a Franco-Indian collaboration with Une Production Florent Paris–Berlin), compositions featuring vocalists such as [[K. S. Chithra|K.S. Chitra]] and [[Kanchana Sisters]], and the ''SPA Sounds'' series of meditation and healing music albums released through Super Audio.[citation needed]

== Teaching ==

Sivaramakrishna Rao runs the '''SRK Academy of Music''' in Bangalore, continuing the [[guru–shishya tradition]] (''parampara''). The academy offers one-on-one sitar instruction in both Hindustani and Carnatic classical traditions, with classes available in-person and online for students worldwide.[citation needed]

He has also released two instructional DVDs, ''Learn To Play Sitar'' (Volumes 1 and 2), covering the history of the sitar, types of sitar, handling, tuning techniques, basic lessons, fingering techniques, and concert patterns.[citation needed]
